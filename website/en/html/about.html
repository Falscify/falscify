About Us

The problem we are trying to solve

The current crisis in science revolves around the issue of publication bias, where only positive results are often favored for publication. This creates a skewed representation of scientific research and can lead to a distortion of knowledge and understanding. By selectively publishing positive results, important but statistically insignificant findings or inconclusive studies are often overlooked, preventing a comprehensive understanding of a particular field. This bias can stem from various factors such as the pressure to publish in high-impact journals, the preference of journals for novel or groundbreaking results, and the tendency of researchers to prioritize positive outcomes.

Our goal

Falscify is here to serve the research community by publishing these so-called negative results. However, our platform is something much more than a database of reports. Falscify is a platform that brings together scientists and allows them to show results that couldn't be published. Our main goal is to show the world that the negative result is as much important as the positive result. By introducing Falscify we want to break the dogma of confirmatory science and enter an era of bias mitigation-focused science.

Mission

As a pioneer of bias mitigation-focused open access publishing, Falscify is based on the following values and guiding principles:
  • Open Access
  • Community
  • Simplicity
  • High Quality Service

Passionate about science

The creators of this platform are two unwavering dreamers who possess a profound love for science. They strongly disagree with the current state of science and are determined to enact change.

Why 'falscify'?

Falscify is derived from the word 'falsify', which means to demonstrate the falsehood of a statement or theory. Our aim is to demonstrate that these results are equally valid. We aspire to provide scientists with a platform where they can freely share such results and leverage the knowledge of others.
